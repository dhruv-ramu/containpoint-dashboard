# SPCC rule overview

The EPA Spill Prevention, Control, and Countermeasure (SPCC) rule at 40 CFR Part 112 applies to facilities that drill, produce, store, process, refine, transfer, distribute, use, or consume oil and could reasonably be expected to discharge oil to navigable waters or adjoining shorelines.

## Applicability thresholds

A facility is subject to SPCC if it has an aggregate aboveground oil storage capacity greater than 1,320 U.S. gallons or completely buried storage capacity greater than 42,000 U.S. gallons, and meets the discharge potential criterion.

## Plan requirements

Covered facilities must prepare and implement an SPCC Plan that describes equipment, practices, and procedures to prevent and control oil discharges. Plans must be reviewed and amended when there are material changes and must undergo a five-year review.

## Qualified facilities

Tier I and Tier II qualified facilities may self-certify their plans if they meet eligibility criteria. Other facilities require certification by a licensed Professional Engineer.
