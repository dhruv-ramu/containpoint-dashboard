# Secondary containment

## Purpose

Secondary containment is designed to prevent oil from reaching navigable waters by containing discharges from the largest single container plus sufficient freeboard, or through alternative measures allowed under the rule when properly engineered and documented.

## Common methods

Dikes, berms, curbing, culverting, weirs, booms, spill diversion ponds, retention ponds, sorbent materials, and other equipment or structures may be used as described in the facility’s SPCC Plan.

## Containment sizing

The plan must explain how containment volume and design meet applicable regulatory requirements for the facility’s configuration.
